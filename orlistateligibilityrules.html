Orlistat — Patient Eligibility & Rules

Oral Weight Loss Consultation — New Patient Pathway

Draft v2 June 2026 For Prescriber Review Orlistat

Deferred eligibility model: The patient completes the full consultation form before any eligibility decision is shown. There are no mid-form blocks.

All rejections are deferred — displayed only at the end, after the full form is submitted.

This document covers the New Patient Orlistat pathway. The full question flow for the Existing Patient (Repeat) Orlistat pathway is a separate document — Step 1 (BMI Eligibility) below also documents the BMI rule that applies to repeat patients.

📝 Developer note — age eligibility (18–75): Do not add a question asking "Are you between the age of 18 and 75?" — this question does not currently exist in the form and should not be added as a separate question. Instead, calculate the patient's age from their date of birth (collected in the BMI measurements step) against the consultation date. If the calculated age falls outside 18–75, treat this the same as any other deferred rejection criteria.

BMI Eligibility
1a — New Patient BMI Rule
⚠ Ethnicity (BAME) has no effect on BMI eligibility for Orlistat. Only the presence of a qualifying comorbidity changes the threshold.
Question
"Have you ever been diagnosed with any of the following?"

Selecting at least one of the following qualifies as a comorbidity for the BMI rule below.

Prediabetes
Type 2 diabetes mellitus (diet controlled)
Hypertension (high blood pressure)
Dyslipidaemia (high cholesterol / raised triglycerides / low HDL)
Cardiovascular disease — including previous heart attack, previous stroke, TIA, ischaemic heart disease, peripheral arterial disease, heart failure
Obstructive sleep apnoea
MASLD / NAFLD (metabolic dysfunction-associated steatotic liver disease)
Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS) with metabolic features
Patient ProfileMinimum BMI
No qualifying comorbidity30.0
One or more qualifying comorbidities28.0

≥ threshold for profile (28 or 30)Eligible
< threshold for profileDeferred Rejection
1b — Repeat / Existing Patient BMI Rule

For patients repeating Orlistat (existing patients), the new-patient threshold (28 / 30) and comorbidity list above do not apply. Instead, a single BMI floor applies regardless of comorbidity or BAME status.

BMIOutcome
Above 21Eligible

ℹ Outside SOP Eligibility is treated the same as a deferred rejection — shown only at the end of the form, after submission.
📝 Developer note: please confirm the exact boundary value with the clinical team — i.e. whether a BMI of exactly 21.0 should count as eligible or as "Outside SOP Eligibility". This document treats 21.0 itself as not eligible (BMI must be strictly above 21).

ℹ Orlistat-specific counselling text (verbatim):

"Orlistat and oral contraception
Orlistat can cause severe diarrhoea. The Orlistat Summary of Product Characteristics (SPC) states that severe diarrhoea may reduce the effectiveness of oral contraceptive pills. If you are prescribed Orlistat, you should use an additional contraceptive method — for example, condoms — particularly if you experience severe diarrhoea while taking it."
